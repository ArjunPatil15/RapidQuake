Emergency Preparedness Guide

Be ready before disaster strikes. Follow these essential steps to protect yourself and your loved ones

1

Create an Emergency Plan

Develop a comprehensive emergency plan with your family. Ensure everyone knows what to do when an earthquake strikes.

Planning Checklist:

Identify safe spots in each room (under sturdy tables, away from windows)
Establish a family meeting point outside your home
Designate an out-of-state contact person
Practice "Drop, Cover, and Hold On" drills regularly
Keep emergency contact numbers accessible to all family members
2

Secure Your Home

Take steps to minimize damage and prevent injuries by securing potential hazards in your home.

Home Safety Checklist:

Bolt heavy furniture and appliances to walls
Install latches on cabinet doors to prevent contents from spilling
Secure water heater with metal straps
Store heavy items on lower shelves
Know how to shut off gas, water, and electricity
3

Build an Emergency Kit

Prepare a disaster supply kit with essential items to last at least 72 hours. Store it in an easily accessible location.

4

Stay Informed

Keep yourself updated with the latest information and alerts about earthquakes in your area.

Information Sources:

Download RapidQuake app for real-time alerts
Sign up for local emergency alert systems
Keep a battery-powered or hand-crank radio
Follow USGS and local emergency management on social media

Emergency Supply Kit Essentials

Water & Food

  • 1 gallon water per person/day (3 days)
  • Non-perishable food (3-day supply)
  • Manual can opener
  • Paper plates, cups, utensils

First Aid

  • First aid kit with instructions
  • Prescription medications (7-day supply)
  • Over-the-counter medicines
  • Medical supplies (glasses, hearing aids)

Clothing & Bedding

  • Change of clothing per person
  • Sturdy shoes or boots
  • Sleeping bag or blanket per person
  • Rain gear and seasonal clothing

Tools & Supplies

  • Battery-powered or hand-crank radio
  • Flashlight with extra batteries
  • Whistle for signaling help
  • Wrench to turn off utilities

Documents

  • Copies of important documents
  • Insurance policies
  • Bank account records
  • Emergency contact list

Communication

  • Cell phone with chargers
  • Portable battery pack
  • Solar-powered charger
  • Two-way radios

Sanitation

  • Toilet paper and towelettes
  • Soap and hand sanitizer
  • Garbage bags and plastic ties
  • Household bleach

Special Needs

  • Infant formula and diapers
  • Pet food and supplies
  • Mobility aids (wheelchair, walker)
  • Comfort items (toys, books)

What to Do: Before, During, After

BEFORE an Earthquake

Practice drills: Conduct earthquake drills with your family every 6 months. Make sure everyone knows to Drop, Cover, and Hold On. Review your emergency plan and update contact information. Check your emergency kit and replace expired items. Secure heavy objects and review evacuation routes from your home and workplace.

DURING an Earthquake

Stay calm and act quickly: Drop down to your hands and knees. Cover your head and neck under a sturdy table or desk. Hold on to your shelter until shaking stops. If no table is nearby, protect your head with your arms. Stay away from windows, outside doors, and walls. If you're outside, move to an open area away from buildings, trees, and power lines. If driving, pull over safely and stay in the vehicle.

AFTER an Earthquake

Check for injuries and damage: Check yourself and others for injuries. Provide first aid as needed. Inspect your home for damage. If the building is unsafe, evacuate immediately. Check for gas leaks, water line damage, and electrical system problems. Turn off utilities if damage is suspected. Use flashlights, not candles. Listen to emergency broadcasts for updates. Be prepared for aftershocks. Help neighbors who may need assistance, especially elderly or disabled individuals.
