03

Secure Your Home & Property

Prevent injuries and minimize damage through proper anchoring

HIGH PRIORITY

Heavy Furniture

Secure bookcases, cabinets, and tall furniture to wall studs using L-brackets and anchor bolts. Top-heavy items are the leading cause of earthquake injuries.

HIGH PRIORITY

Water Heater

Strap water heaters to wall studs using metal straps (top and bottom). This prevents tipping, which can rupture gas lines and cause fires.

Kitchen Safety

Install latches on cabinet doors to prevent dishes from falling out. Store heavy items on lower shelves.

Electronics

Use earthquake-resistant straps or adhesive putty for TVs, computers, and other electronics.

Utilities Knowledge

Know the location of utility shutoffs and how to operate them safely.

Wall Decorations

Use closed hooks for hanging pictures and mirrors, especially over beds and seating areas.

04

Conduct Regular Safety Drills

Practice makes perfect - drill every 6 months minimum

1

Drop, Cover, and Hold On Drill

Practice the proper earthquake response: Drop to hands and knees, take Cover under sturdy furniture, and Hold On until shaking stops. Time goal: React within 3 seconds.

2

Evacuation Drill

Practice evacuating to designated meeting points. Each family member should know two routes from every room and reach the meeting point within 5 minutes.

3

Communication Check

Test your communication plan. Have each family member check in with the designated out-of-state contact using text messages (texts work better than calls during disasters).

4

Utility Shutoff Practice

Ensure all adults know how to shut off gas, water, and electricity. Practice locating shutoff valves and using the proper tools. Never turn gas back on yourself - call utility company.

Only practice locating valves - DO NOT actually shut off utilities during drill

Recommended Drill Schedule

Monthly Drop, Cover, Hold On
Quarterly Full Evacuation Drill
Semi-Annually Complete Emergency Plan Review
05

Review Insurance Coverage

Ensure adequate financial protection for earthquake damage

Important: Standard homeowners insurance does NOT cover earthquake damage. You need separate earthquake insurance.

Earthquake Insurance

  • Research earthquake insurance providers
  • Get quotes from multiple insurers
  • Understand deductible options (typically 10-25%)
  • Review coverage limits for dwelling and contents
  • Consider additional living expense coverage

Document Your Property

  • Create video walkthrough of all rooms
  • Photograph all valuable items
  • Keep receipts for major purchases
  • Store documentation in cloud/offsite
  • Update inventory annually

During an Earthquake: Immediate Actions

1

DROP

Drop to your hands and knees immediately. This protects you from being knocked down and allows you to crawl to shelter.

2

COVER

Take cover under a sturdy desk, table, or against an interior wall. Cover your head and neck with your arms.

3

HOLD ON

Hold on to your shelter and be prepared to move with it. Stay in place until shaking stops and it's safe to exit.
