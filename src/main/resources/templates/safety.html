Earthquake Safety Guide

Your Complete Guide to Staying Safe Before, During, and After an Earthquake

During an Earthquake: DROP, COVER, and HOLD ON!

This is the most important action you can take. Drop to your hands and knees, cover your head and neck, and hold on until the shaking stops.

Before an Earthquake

Preparation is key to survival. Take these steps now to protect yourself and your family.

Secure Your Home

  • Anchor heavy furniture like bookcases, cabinets, and water heaters to wall studs
  • Install safety latches on cabinet doors to prevent contents from spilling
  • Store heavy items on lower shelves and secure breakables
  • Check your home's structure for weaknesses and make necessary repairs
  • Know how to shut off gas, water, and electricity in your home
  • Repair defective wiring and leaky gas connections - these can cause fires

Prepare Emergency Supplies

  • Water: 1 gallon per person per day for at least 3 days
  • Food: Non-perishable food for at least 3 days
  • First aid kit with essential medications
  • Flashlight and batteries (avoid candles due to fire risk)
  • Battery-powered or hand-crank radio
  • Cell phone with chargers and backup battery
  • Whistle to signal for help
  • Dust masks, plastic sheeting, and duct tape

Create a Family Plan

  • Establish a meeting place outside your home and neighborhood
  • Designate an out-of-state contact person for family members to call
  • Practice Drop, Cover, and Hold On drills with your family
  • Identify safe spots in every room (under sturdy tables, against interior walls)
  • Keep important documents in a waterproof container
  • Plan for pets and service animals

Educate Yourself

  • Learn about earthquake risks in your area
  • Understand building codes and retrofit requirements
  • Take a first aid and CPR course
  • Know your workplace emergency procedures
  • Sign up for RapidQuake alerts for real-time warnings
  • Participate in earthquake drills like ShakeOut

During an Earthquake

Quick action can save your life. Follow these guidelines based on your location.

DROP

Drop to your hands and knees before the earthquake knocks you down

COVER

Cover your head and neck with your arms. Crawl under a sturdy desk or table if nearby

HOLD ON

Hold on to your shelter and be ready to move with it until shaking stops

If Indoors

  • Stay inside - do NOT run outside
  • Get under a desk or table and hold on
  • Stay away from windows, mirrors, and heavy objects
  • If in bed, stay there and cover your head with a pillow
  • If in a doorway, only use it if you know it's strongly supported
  • In a high-rise, stay away from windows and outside walls

If Outdoors

  • Move away from buildings, power lines, and trees
  • Drop to the ground and cover your head and neck
  • Stay in the open until shaking stops
  • Watch for falling debris from buildings
  • Be aware of landslides if on a slope
  • Stay clear of overhead hazards

If in a Vehicle

  • Pull over safely and stop
  • Avoid bridges, overpasses, and power lines
  • Stay inside with your seatbelt on
  • Cover your head below window level
  • Wait until shaking stops before resuming driving
  • Drive carefully and watch for road damage

What NOT to Do:

  • Do NOT run outside during shaking
  • Do NOT stand in doorways (unless you know they're reinforced)
  • Do NOT use elevators
  • Do NOT light matches or turn on switches (gas leaks may cause explosions)

After an Earthquake

The danger isn't over. Follow these steps to ensure continued safety.

Immediate Actions (First Hour)

  • Check yourself and others for injuries - provide first aid
  • Look for hazards - fire, gas leaks, damaged electrical wiring
  • If you smell gas, open windows, shut off the main valve, and exit
  • Turn off utilities if damage is suspected
  • Inspect your home for structural damage
  • Expect aftershocks - Drop, Cover, Hold On each time
  • Stay out of damaged buildings
  • Use text messages instead of calls (less congestion)

Communication & Coordination

  • Contact your family using your emergency plan
  • Listen to emergency broadcasts on radio
  • Follow official instructions from authorities
  • Check on neighbors who may need help
  • Stay informed via RapidQuake app for aftershock alerts
  • Do not spread rumors - rely on official sources
  • Register your status with Safe and Well registry

Property & Safety Inspection

  • Document damage with photos for insurance
  • Check chimney for cracks before using fireplace
  • Inspect utilities: water, gas, and electrical systems
  • Clean up spills of hazardous materials carefully
  • Open cabinets carefully - contents may fall
  • Stay clear of damaged areas until cleared by professionals
  • Wear protective gear when cleaning up debris

Health & Well-being

  • Drink safe water - bottled or boiled if tap water is unsafe
  • Eat emergency food supplies first
  • Maintain hygiene to prevent illness
  • Watch for emotional trauma - seek counseling if needed
  • Get adequate rest during recovery
  • Continue medications as prescribed
  • Protect yourself from mosquitoes if water has accumulated

Recovery Timeline:

First 72 hours: Focus on safety, first aid, and securing your environment

First week: Begin cleanup, contact insurance, start damage assessment

First month: Work on repairs, seek assistance programs, maintain emergency preparedness

Long-term: Complete repairs, update emergency plans, participate in community resilience efforts
